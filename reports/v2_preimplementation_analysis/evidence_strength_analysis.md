# Evidence Strength Analysis

## Evidence Levels

- Level 1: Keyword mention.
- Level 2: Worked near search.
- Level 3: Built search/retrieval/recommendation features.
- Level 4: Owned ranking/retrieval systems.
- Level 5: Owned search quality/evaluation at scale.

## Level Distribution By Gold Label

| Gold Label | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 | Level 4 | Level 5 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| ELITE | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 11 |
| STRONG | 0 | 0 | 3 | 26 | 10 |
| MEDIUM | 0 | 2 | 3 | 29 | 10 |
| WEAK | 0 | 0 | 5 | 0 | 0 |

## Interpretation

ELITE and STRONG candidates are concentrated in Levels 4 and 5: ownership of ranking/retrieval systems and evaluation/search-quality work at scale. MEDIUM and WEAK candidates more often fall into lower levels or have evidence discounted because it is repeated/template-like, recommendation-only, or not tied to evaluation.

The committee's implicit rule is clear: mentioning search is not enough. V2 should reward ownership, scale, evaluation, and source diversity.
