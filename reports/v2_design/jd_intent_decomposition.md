# JD Intent Decomposition

## Executive Read

The JD is not asking for a generic Senior AI Engineer. It is asking for a production search/relevance/ranking engineer who can own Redrob's candidate-JD intelligence layer.

The best candidate is someone who has shipped ranking, retrieval, matching, or recommendation systems to real users, understands evaluation, and can operate pragmatically in a startup product environment.

## Primary Intent

Primary hiring target:

Search/relevance/ranking engineer with production ownership of candidate matching, retrieval, ranking, or recommendation systems.

Evidence from the JD:

- The role's high-level mandate is to own the intelligence layer: ranking, retrieval, and matching systems deciding what recruiters and candidates see.
- The first 90 days include auditing the current BM25/rule-based system, shipping a V2 ranker, improving recruiter-engagement metrics, and setting up evaluation infrastructure.
- The ideal profile includes shipped end-to-end ranking, search, or recommendation systems at real-user scale.

Primary capability requirements:

- Ranking and relevance systems
- Retrieval systems
- Matching systems
- Product-facing ML engineering
- Evaluation frameworks
- Production code ownership
- Ability to ship an imperfect but useful ranker quickly

Primary candidate families:

- Search Ranking Engineer
- Search/Relevance Applied Scientist
- Retrieval Infrastructure Engineer with ranking exposure
- Recommendation Systems Engineer with ranking/matching/evaluation ownership
- Product ML Engineer who has shipped candidate/job/product/content ranking systems

## Secondary Intent

Secondary target:

Candidates with strong adjacent experience that can transfer directly into Redrob's ranking and retrieval roadmap.

Secondary dimensions:

- Vector search and embeddings
- Hybrid retrieval
- LLM-based reranking or integration
- Recommendation systems
- Product-company applied ML
- Feature pipelines for ranking/retrieval
- Recruiter workflow/product thinking

Evidence from the JD:

- It names embeddings, retrieval, ranking, LLMs, and fine-tuning as the technical depth mix.
- It says V2 will likely involve embeddings, hybrid retrieval, and possibly LLM-based reranking.
- It values strong opinions about hybrid versus dense retrieval, offline versus online evaluation, and when to fine-tune versus prompt.

Secondary candidate families:

- Vector Search Engineer
- NLP Engineer focused on semantic retrieval
- Applied ML Engineer with production recommender/search experience
- Data/ML Infrastructure Engineer whose pipelines feed ranking, retrieval, or feedback systems

## Nice-To-Have Intent

Nice-to-have signals:

- Specific tools: Pinecone, Weaviate, Qdrant, Milvus, OpenSearch, Elasticsearch, FAISS, sentence-transformers, BGE, E5, or similar.
- LLM/fine-tuning experience.
- MLOps experience.
- Strong Python and production code quality.
- Mentoring and team scaling experience.
- Startup experience and comfort with evolving requirements.

Important interpretation:

Tool names are not the job. They are evidence only when tied to production systems, operational responsibility, quality metrics, or shipped product impact.

The JD explicitly says the specific vector/search technology matters less than operational experience. V2 should therefore reward tool names less than proof of system ownership.

## Explicit Warnings

Warning 1: AI keyword stuffing

The JD says the correct answer is not finding candidates whose skills section contains the most AI keywords.

V2 implication:

- Skill-only evidence must be capped.
- Career descriptions should dominate.
- Repeated keyword bundles should add little after the first few source-diverse matches.

Warning 2: AI transition candidates

The JD explicitly gives the example of a Marketing Manager with AI keywords as not a fit.

V2 implication:

- Non-ML or unrelated current titles with AI skill lists need strong career proof to recover.
- Recent LangChain/OpenAI-only experience should not rank highly without pre-existing production ML/search/retrieval experience.

Warning 3: Pure research without deployment

The JD says pure research environments without production deployment are a disqualifier.

V2 implication:

- Research-only evidence should be supporting, not primary.
- Product deployment and operational ownership should be explicit feature dimensions.

Warning 4: Stable-ladder big-company expectations

The JD indicates this is a startup role, not a well-scoped ladder role.

V2 implication:

- Reward candidates with shipping velocity, product ambiguity, and practical ownership.
- Do not over-rank profiles that are strong only in narrow research or maintenance roles.

Warning 5: Behavioral unavailability

The JD says a perfect-on-paper candidate who has not logged in for months and has very low recruiter response is not actually available for hiring purposes.

V2 implication:

- Behavioral signals matter.
- They should act as availability/tie-break features, not as a substitute for relevance.

## What The JD Is Truly Trying To Hire

The JD is trying to hire a founding senior AI/search engineer who can:

1. Audit an existing BM25/rule-based ranking system.
2. Identify the highest-leverage relevance improvements.
3. Ship a better ranker quickly.
4. Improve recruiter-engagement metrics.
5. Build retrieval/ranking evaluation infrastructure.
6. Drive long-term candidate-JD matching architecture.
7. Work closely with product/recruiter workflows.

This is closer to a Search/Relevance/Ranking Engineer than a generic AI Engineer.

## Target Candidate Definition For V2

Ideal V2 target:

A product-oriented search/relevance engineer with 5-9 years of experience, multiple years of applied ML/search/recommendation ownership, shipped ranking or retrieval systems to real users, hands-on evaluation experience, and enough behavioral availability to be reachable.

Acceptable high-rank adjacent profiles:

- Recommendation Systems Engineer who built ranking/matching systems at a product company.
- Retrieval Infrastructure Engineer who owned search indexes, hybrid retrieval, and quality regression.
- NLP Engineer whose NLP work directly powered search, semantic retrieval, or reranking.
- Applied ML Engineer whose career descriptions prove ranker/retrieval/evaluation ownership.

Lower-rank profiles:

- Generic AI Engineer with LLM/RAG skills but no ranking/retrieval career evidence.
- Generic ML Engineer with production models but no search/recommendation/matching relevance.
- Data Engineer with pipelines but no search/relevance connection.
- Consulting AI profile with prototypes but no production product ownership.
- AI-transition profile with unrelated career history.

## Implications For V2 Ranking Philosophy

1. Career evidence should remain more trusted than skills.
2. Ranking and retrieval ownership should outrank broad AI skill breadth.
3. Recommendation experience should be high value only when it includes ranking/matching/evaluation.
4. Evaluation experience should become an explicit feature family, not an incidental keyword.
5. Product deployment should be a major trust signal.
6. Behavioral availability should break ties and downweight dormant profiles, not dominate technical relevance.
7. Consistency and suspicious-profile signals should cap high-risk candidates rather than merely subtract small points.
