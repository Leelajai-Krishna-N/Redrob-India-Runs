# Top 100 Quality Review

## Direct Answers

1. The candidates are generally relevant to the Search/Relevance/Ranking problem. Top 100 candidates have high ranking and retrieval career-evidence rates.
2. Common patterns: high technical score, saturated/near-saturated career score, explicit ranking/retrieval evidence, and strong market scores.
3. Common skills include BM25, OpenSearch, Elasticsearch, Learning to Rank, Information Retrieval, Semantic Search, Vector Search, Embeddings, and vector databases.
4. Common titles include Recommendation Systems Engineer, Search Engineer, Senior Data Scientist, Machine Learning Engineer, Applied ML Engineer, NLP Engineer, and AI Engineer.
5. Experience is mid-to-senior, but raw years alone does not dominate.
6. Retrieval candidates are strongly represented.
7. Ranking candidates are strongly represented.
8. Recommendation candidates are visibly overrepresented by title.

## Staff-Level Assessment

V1 is not merely selecting generic AI keyword-heavy profiles: Top 100 keyword stuffer and AI-transition flags are both zero. However, recommendation-system candidates may be overrepresented because recommendation titles and skills co-occur with ranking/retrieval terms and `career_score` can saturate. V2 should distinguish direct search ranking, recommender ranking, retrieval infrastructure, and generic ML more explicitly.
